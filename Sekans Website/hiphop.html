HipHop

Hip-hop dance is a dynamic and high-energy style of movement that originated in the United States during the late 1960s and early 1970s. It emerged as part of hip-hop culture, which includes elements like DJing, rapping (MCing), graffiti art, and breaking (breakdancing). Hip-hop dance is a vibrant and energetic street dance style that emerged in the 1970s in the Bronx, New York City. Rooted in hip-hop culture, it combines rhythmic movements, improvisation, and creativity, influenced by breaking, popping, locking, and freestyle techniques. Performed to upbeat hip-hop music, this dance form celebrates individuality, self-expression, and community. Today, it thrives worldwide in battles, performances, and as a key element of pop culture. Hip-hop dance is not just movement; it’s a celebration of passion, rhythm, and urban artistry.
