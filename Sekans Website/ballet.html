Ballet

Ballet originated during the Italian Renaissance in the 15th century and was later refined in France and Russia. The term "ballet" comes from the Italian word balleto, meaning to dance. Ballet is a graceful and highly disciplined dance form that originated during the Italian Renaissance in the 15th century and later developed in France and Russia. Known for its precision, elegance, and emotional expression, ballet combines flowing movements, intricate footwork, and dramatic storytelling. Dancers train rigorously to perform challenging techniques like pirouettes, leaps, and pointe work, often set to classical music. With its iconic costumes, including tutus and tights, ballet has become a cornerstone of both classical and contemporary performance arts, captivating audiences worldwide.
